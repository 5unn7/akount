**Goal:** Replace TASKS.md with proper PM tool for strategic planning, sprint management, and metrics.

---

## TL;DR — Recommendation

**For Akount:** Use **Linear**

**Why:**

## Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Linear (⭐ RECOMMENDED)

**Pros:**
- ✅ **Fastest UI** — keyboard shortcuts, instant search, no lag
- ✅ **Git-native** — auto-link commits, auto-close on merge
- ✅ **Beautiful design** — actually enjoyable to use
- ✅ **Built-in metrics** — velocity, cycle time, burndown (no setup)
- ✅ **Cycles** — 2-week sprints with goals and auto-archiving
- ✅ **Teams** — perfect for your 9 domains (Banking, Invoicing, etc.)
- ✅ **Projects** — map to your epics (NUJ UX Overhaul, etc.)
- ✅ **Free tier** — 250 issues (perfect for your 256 tasks)
- ✅ **API** — clean SDK, easy to sync with TASKS.md

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ No time tracking (use Toggl/Clockify if needed)
- ⚠️ No custom fields on free tier (upgrade to Standard for $8/mo)

**Best For:** Software teams prioritizing velocity and developer experience

**Pricing:**
- Free: Up to 250 issues (PERFECT for you)
- Standard: $8/user/month (unlimited issues, custom fields)
- Plus: $14/user/month (advanced analytics, automations)

---

### 2. Height

**Pros:**
- ✅ Very fast, clean UI
- ✅ Built-in AI assistant (auto-categorize, suggest next steps)
- ✅ Chat-based collaboration (comments + chat in one)
- ✅ Spreadsheet view (like Notion tables)

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Free tier: Only 50 tasks (you have 256 — need paid)
- ⚠️ Newer tool (launched 2022 — less mature)
- ⚠️ Smaller community, fewer integrations

**Best For:** Small teams wanting AI assistance

**Pricing:**
- Free: 50 tasks (TOO SMALL)
- Business: $8/user/month

---

### 3. Notion

**Pros:**
- ✅ Most flexible (databases, docs, wikis, kanban all in one)
- ✅ Rich editing (embeds, images, callouts)
- ✅ Templates (lots of PM templates available)
- ✅ Free tier: Unlimited pages/tasks

**Cons:**
- ❌ **Slow** — especially with large databases
- ❌ **Not built for software** — no velocity, burndown, story points
- ❌ **Weak Git integration** — no auto-link commits
- ⚠️ No native timeline/roadmap view
- ⚠️ API is limited (hard to sync reliably)

**Best For:** Content/docs-heavy teams, wikis, knowledge bases

**Pricing:**
- Free: Unlimited individual use
- Plus: $10/user/month (teams)

---

### 4. Jira

**Pros:**
- ✅ Industry standard (most features)
- ✅ Advanced workflows (custom states, automations)
- ✅ Powerful reporting (custom queries, dashboards)
- ✅ Enterprise-grade (compliance, audit logs)

**Cons:**
- ❌ **Terrible UI** — slow, confusing, overwhelming
- ❌ **Steep learning curve** — takes weeks to learn
- ❌ **Expensive** — no free tier, starts at $8/user
- ❌ **Overkill** — designed for 100+ person teams

**Best For:** Large enterprises, regulated industries

**Pricing:**
- Free: None (trial only)
- Standard: $8.15/user/month (min 1 user)
- Premium: $16/user/month

---

### 5. ClickUp

**Pros:**
- ✅ Most features (everything in one tool)
- ✅ Time tracking built-in
- ✅ Custom fields, automations, goals

**Cons:**
- ❌ **Bloated** — too many features, hard to navigate
- ❌ **Slow** — UI lags with large task lists
- ⚠️ Free tier: 100 tasks (you'd need paid)
- ⚠️ Not optimized for software teams

**Best For:** Agencies, general project management

**Pricing:**
- Free: 100 tasks (TOO SMALL)
- Unlimited: $9/user/month

## Decision Matrix

### Use Linear if:
- ✅ You want velocity/burndown/cycle time tracking
- ✅ You prioritize speed and clean UI
- ✅ You want git-native integration
- ✅ Your tasks are software-focused
- ✅ You're solo or small team (1-5 people)

### Use Notion if:
- You need rich docs/wikis alongside tasks
- You want maximum flexibility (custom databases)
- Speed isn't critical
- You don't need velocity tracking

### Use Jira if:
- You're a 50+ person team
- You need enterprise compliance
- You have dedicated admins to manage it
- Budget isn't a concern

### Use Height if:
- You want AI-assisted planning
- You're willing to pay from day 1
- You want a Linear alternative with unique features

### Use ClickUp if:
- You need time tracking + PM in one tool
- You manage non-software projects too
- You want "everything in one place" (at cost of speed)
